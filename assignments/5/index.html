As seen from the bar chart above the number of robberies that occurred on a given weekday all lie in the interval $[3100, 3500]$. The weekend has more robberies than normal with Saturday being the day where one is most likely to be robbed and Monday being the least likely. The surprising fact about this graph is that the day of the week has little impact on the likelihood of being robbed, we suspected that one would be more likely to be robbed Friday or Saturday. Which is not the case the probability that someone is robbed on a given Saturday is $\frac{3434}{3\cdot 365} * 100 = 78.40\%$ and the probability of it happing on a monday is $\frac{3198}{3\cdot 365} * 100 = 73.01\%$ which is a small difference.
