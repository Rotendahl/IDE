Urbanisation in Denmark

The population in a country is rarely steady. As opportunities arise in the cities and the countryside offers fewer jobs it is common for people to move towards the cities. In Denmark anno 1801 almost 80 % of the population lived in the countryside but in 2004 more than 85 % were found in cities. This is a massive shift over 200 years. What we want to show is that the population have not stabilized. People are still moving and small towns are suffering from businesses closing and jobs moving away. In recent years we have seen attempts at revitalizing "udkantsdanmark" (outer Denmark) by moving government jobs away from Copenhagen. We have made a visualization of the population movement in recent years. The map allows you to zoom by scrolling and you can also drag the map. Below is a slider where you can choose an interval of two years to see the population change between them. The slider can be a bit wonky.

A dark blue indicates a large decrease in population and red is a large increase in population. The values on the legend bar updates as the interval is changed.
     As the map shows the danish population is gathering around the cities, the capital of Copenhagen has seen a large increase the smaller cities such as Esbjerg, Vejle etc have also seen an increase.
     We opted for percentage difference instead of the actual difference or exact population firstly because the muncipialities vary greatly in size and secondly because larger populations see larger growth in numbers without people moving. If we had population densities we could show some changes in this, but it would not be showing the urbanization.
